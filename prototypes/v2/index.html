Which of these teams had the best championship season?

Method 01: Most wins in season

One way to look for the best championship season is to compare how many races each constructor's drivers won in their championship runs. In Formula One, as in other racing sports, finishing first is the holy grail. The best championship run for a constructor would be one where their team wins every race.

Methodology

For each season:

  1. Find the driver that won the year's Driver's Cup.
  2. Look at what constructor he was driving for
  3. See how many races the constructor's drivers finished first in.
  4. Divide by the number of races in the season to get the percentage of races won.

Overall Mercedes and Ferrari championship runs dominate the list of teams who won the most races.

In 1988, McLaren became the closest team in modern Formula One to have a golden season. McLaren drivers Alain Prost and Ayrton Senna won every race of the season except for the Italian Grand Prix.

either headshots for senna and prost or a photo of the mclaren 1988 car

In that race, Senna and Prost started first and second on the grid, but Prost was forced to retire after 34 laps due to engine problems. With two laps to go and while leading the race, Senna was also forced to retire due to a collision with Williams driver Jean-Louis Schlesser.

In spite of the loss at the Italian Grand Prix, Prost and Senna won 7 and 8 races respectively, and Senna won his first of three Driver's Cup Championships.

The other two teams that have come close to having a golden season are Ferrari in 1952 (ranked fourth) and Alfa Romeo in 1950 (ranked fifth). Ferrari competed in every race that season, but was not able to win the year's Indianapolis 500. On the other hand, Alfa Romeo won every race they participated in, but they did not try to enter the 1950 Indianapolis 500.

Close behind McLaren's 1988 run are Mercedes's 2016 and Ferrari's 2002 runs.

In 2016, Mercedes extended their dominating lead of the hybrid-engine era by winning 19 of the 21 races in the season.

Ahead of the 2014 season Formula One's governing body, the FIA, changed several regulations to stimulate competition and the design of the cars. One of the main changes was the move away from 2.4-litre V8 engines to 1.6-litre V6 turbo engines.

Mercedes was able to capitulate on the new design regulations and create a car, the Mercedes F1 W05 Hybrid, that enabled Lewis Hamilton and Nico Rosberg to dominate in the 2014 season, winning 16 of the season's 19 races (rank 6, tie).

photo of the mercedes w05

In 2015, Mercedes was again able to leverage their strong car and Hamilton and Rosberg again won 16 of the season's race (rank 6, tie).

Behind them is Ferrari's 2002 run. In 2002, Michael Schumacher won his fifth Driver's Cup while driving with Rubens Barrichello for Ferrari. The 2002 season is marked by the success of the F2002, a very robust Formula 1 car.

photo of the ferrari f2002

While it was still in development for the first two races of the season, Schumacher and Barrichello won 14 of their 15 season wins in the F2002. And with the exception of two races, both drivers finished in the top three for every race they had in the car.

Ferrari's 2004 run (ranked 8), was another season where Schumacher and Barrichello had a stellar performance, this time in the Ferrari F2004. While driving the car, Schumacher also set a record for most season wins at 13 wins and also won his seventh and final Driver's Cup.

Ferrari's other two runs in the top 10 were in 1952 (ranked 4) and 1953 (ranked 9) when the Formula One races were held to Formula Two standards and the Ferrari 500 was the best car to race in.
